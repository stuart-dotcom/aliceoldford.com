Florida on Land and Water — FAPA Gold Medal

10 Adventures and More in Lee & Collier Counties

Where can you enjoy an active outdoor experience in bustling Lee and Collier Counties in Florida? Interested in learning some local history?

How did the Monday Group, a bunch of ‘70s teenagers, influence development of Six Mile Cypress Slough Preserve and Manatee Park? Who were the utopian Koreshans? Try a family outing at Everglades Wonder Gardens, formerly a roadside attraction in the heart of Bonita Springs. Experience Florida scrub at Hickey’s Creek Mitigation Park. And there is water, water everywhere — perfect for a leisurely paddle.

Adventure right here in your own back yard.

Food & Life

Recipes and Life: Life is Too Short to Be Stuck in the Kitchen

“One way to get the most out of life is to look upon it as an adventure.” — William Feather

Eating — a habit, a pleasure — guided by the seasons of the year and of our lives.

Do you want to liven up your family’s meals, maximize your food budget, and limit your time in the kitchen? Do you have one or more picky eaters? Is dieting on someone’s agenda? What can you do to make leftovers appealing or change up old favorites? Looking to avoid the processed food pitfalls, but think you don’t have time?

This is Alice’s collection of inspirations sprinkled with favorite recipes — practical tips gleaned from a chef and friends who have agreed to share their recipes and insights. Whether you live alone or with others, are a meat-eater, vegetarian, or a dieter, if you are willing to think outside the box, these thoughts are for you.

Get There From Here

You Can Get There From Here — Hiking Hunterdon County Trails

Embracing Hunterdon and surrounding counties — oases in western central New Jersey, the most densely populated state in the United States — inspired You Can Get There from Here – Hiking Hunterdon County Trails and its sequel Hiking NJ Trails – Hunterdon County and Beyond.

Hunterdon, Warren and Morris counties are part of the Skylands Region bounded on the west by the Delaware River with a view of Pennsylvania. Farm fields, forests, hills, wildlife, and four reservoirs — as well as historic homes, farms and estates — describe a landscape home to parks and trails offering outdoor opportunities to folks of all ages.

The first book, co-authored with Sue Dziamara, grew from walking and exploring Hunterdon County and wishing to share the wealth. The sequel broadens the quest, extending to Highlands trails and routes outside of Hunterdon County. Enjoy the trails year round and do some exploring on your own!

Trail routes are also featured on MetroTrails and the Hunterdon County trail network.
